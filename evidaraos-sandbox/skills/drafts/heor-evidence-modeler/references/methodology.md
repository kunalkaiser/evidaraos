# HEOR Evidence Modeler Methodology Notes

Use these sources as methodological anchors when building or reviewing outputs.

## Reporting and Model Practice Anchors

- CHEERS 2022: reporting guideline for health economic evaluations. Use as a reporting completeness checklist for economic evidence summaries. Source: https://www.ispor.org/heor-resources/good-practices/cheers
- ISPOR-SMDM Modeling Good Research Practices: emphasizes model conceptualization, implementation choices, uncertainty, validation, and transparent reporting. Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22990082/
- ISPOR-SMDM model transparency and validation: use for validation/readiness checks and model documentation expectations. Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22990088/
- ISPOR budget impact analysis good practice: budget impact analyses should reflect the decision-maker population, current and new treatment mix, treatment/resource costs, condition-related costs, uptake, and scenario/sensitivity analyses. Source: https://www.ispor.org/publications/journals/value-in-health/abstract/Volume-17--Issue-1/budget-impact-analysis-principles-of-good-practice-report-of-the-ispor-2012-budget-impact-analysis-good-practice-ii-task-force

## Evidence Input Classes

Extract separately:

- health-state utilities
- adverse-event disutilities
- event rates
- mortality rates
- treatment discontinuation
- resource utilization
- unit costs
- administration and monitoring costs
- comparator mix
- market share / uptake assumptions
- eligible population assumptions

## Required Quality Checks

- Is the perspective clear?
- Is the population aligned to the model decision problem?
- Is each input sourced or explicitly marked as an assumption?
- Are uncertainty ranges available?
- Is the value usable in sensitivity/scenario analysis?
- Is the time horizon clear?
- Is the model type appropriate for the decision question?
- Has a health economist reviewed the input?

## Prohibited Behavior

- Do not invent model inputs.
- Do not present modeled estimates as final economic results.
- Do not present ICER, QALY, or budget impact outputs without an actual model and validation.
- Do not hide assumptions.
