# Regulatory Evidence Briefing Methodology Notes

Use these sources as methodological anchors when building or reviewing regulatory briefing outputs.

## FDA Benefit-Risk Anchor

FDA describes benefit-risk assessment as part of drug and biologic regulatory decision-making. FDA's framework organizes evidence, uncertainty, and reasoning around therapeutic context, available treatment options, benefits, risks/risk management, and patient experience information where relevant.

Sources:

- FDA benefit-risk assessment page: https://www.fda.gov/industry/prescription-drug-user-fee-amendments/enhancing-benefit-risk-assessment-regulatory-decision-making
- FDA final guidance, Benefit-Risk Assessment for New Drug and Biological Products, October 2023: https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/benefit-risk-assessment-new-drug-and-biological-products
- FDA guidance recap podcast page: https://www.fda.gov/drugs/guidances-drugs/guidance-recap-podcast-benefit-risk-assessment-new-drug-and-biologic-products

## EMA Anchor

EMA states that the balance between benefits and risks is the key principle guiding medicine assessment, and risk minimization/monitoring after authorization is part of the assessment.

Source:

- https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/about-us/what-we-do/authorisation-medicines/how-ema-evaluates-medicines-human-use

## Briefing Structure

Use:

- therapeutic context
- current treatment options
- benefit evidence
- risk evidence
- risk management / minimization
- patient experience, if available
- uncertainty
- precedent relevance
- source appendix
- human regulatory review status

## Prohibited Behavior

- Do not predict regulatory approval.
- Do not overstate precedent relevance.
- Do not say evidence proves safety or efficacy.
- Do not produce final submission language without regulatory expert review.
