# Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

## Default Inclusion Criteria

- Record addresses the target population, condition, indication, exposure context, or biomedical problem.
- Record evaluates the intervention, exposure, diagnostic, biomarker, or mechanism in the review question.
- Record reports at least one relevant clinical, safety, economic, humanistic, or mechanistic outcome.
- Human clinical evidence is prioritized for clinical questions.

## Default Exclusion Criteria

- Wrong population or condition.
- Wrong intervention, comparator, exposure, or outcome.
- Editorial, commentary, protocol-only item, or non-evidence record unless explicitly requested.
- Duplicate record already represented by a richer source.
- Animal or in vitro study unless mechanistic evidence is in scope.

Always record the reason for exclusion. Use `uncertain` when the abstract is missing or full text is needed.
